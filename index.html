Bridging Silence with
Hybrid Intelligence

A dual-mode communication interface that translates sign language into synthetic speech using TinyML and Fail-Safe Discrete Logic.

I. The Core Problem Statement

Approximately 70 million deaf people worldwide use sign language as their first language. However, the communication gap between signers and non-signers creates massive barriers in healthcare, employment, and emergency services.

Critical Failure of Current Solutions: Most smart gloves rely purely on AI. If the hand is in a shadow or the sensor glitches, the translation stops. NeuralVoice introduces "Manual Priority" to solve this.

II. AI Neural Engine & Sensory Fusion

Our system doesn't just "read" sensors; it understands context. By using Sensor Fusion, we combine data from three distinct sources:

6-Axis

Inertial Motion (MPU6050) for tracking hand trajectory.

5-Point

Hall Effect Magnetic Flux to track finger curvature.

4-Bit

Binary Touch Pads for high-priority overrides.

The ESP32-S3 runs an Edge Impulse model trained on thousands of samples. This allows the glove to detect movement patterns (gestures) and static shapes (finger positions) simultaneously.

III. Massive Usability & Global Impact

How this device changes lives in real-world scenarios:

1. Medical Emergency Autonomy

In a hospital setting, a deaf patient can use the "Manual Priority" mode to immediately trigger words like PAIN or DOCTOR. This bypasses any AI learning curve and works 100% of the time.

2. Socio-Economic Empowerment

This glove allows speech-impaired individuals to take jobs in customer service. By providing a clear, loud voice output through the Arduino-driven speaker, the user can interact with customers seamlessly.

3. Educational Integration

The device acts as a real-time tutor, helping children learn sign language by giving them instant audio feedback when they perform the sign correctly.
